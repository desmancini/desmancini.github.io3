URL
Increase in Depressive Symptoms Linked to Technology
Site Author
 Jean M. Twenge


Abstract
 This website contains national statistics on the rise of depressive symptoms and suicide rates in adolescents ages thirteen to eighteen. It studies how the suicide rates among this age group have increased between 2010 and 2015. The rise in depressive symptoms and suicide could possibly be linked to technology and the amount of screen time that this age group engages in. Adolescents who reported that they spent more time on technology such as scrolling on social media rather than nonscreen activities such as sports were more likely to also report mental health issues. The site further explains how the statistics were collected and the significance the results provide.



URL
 How Technology Affects Sleep
Site Author
 Dr. Michael Breus
Abstract
 This website explores how technology use can affect sleep patterns. The most significant points that the article provides is how technology can disrupt melatonin production and circadian rhythms, screen time before bed can increase stress and mental stimulation, and the importance of quality sleep for child development. The article also provides tips on how to create a bedtime routine excluding screen time to result in healthier sleeping habits. Some tips that were provided are turning off notifications, enforcing a no screen rule close to childrens bedtime, establishing rules for the use of technology in bed, finding a relaxing activity to do before bed that does not include technology, and more.
